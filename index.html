Daniel Ige – GRC Journey

Hi, my name is Daniel Ige. I specialize in Governance, Risk, and Compliance. This portfolio showcases my technical foundations, audit experience, certifications, and the projects that demonstrate my readiness for real-world security challenges.

About Me

My journey into cybersecurity did not begin with firewalls or network logs, it began with a deep commitment to protecting people, data, and the systems organizations rely on every day.

I began my career as an epidemiologist, where I analyzed large datasets, identified risk patterns, and supported public health interventions through statistical modeling. This work taught me how to handle sensitive information responsibly and how to communicate insights that influence critical decisions.

As a data manager, I deepened my understanding of data governance. I built departmental databases, developed reporting dashboards, established data standards, and participated in HIPAA compliance activities. This role strengthened my appreciation for secure data practices, quality management, and the policies that govern information flow.

Most recently, as a grant manager overseeing more than $6 million in awards, I worked heavily in compliance, oversight, and internal controls. I coordinated spending activities, conducted fraud, risk, and vendor due diligence, ensured regulatory alignment, and created dashboards and reports for leadership and auditors. It was in this role that I realized how much I enjoyed governance, documentation, risk assessment, and the processes that hold organizations accountable.

These experiences collectively inspired my pivot into cybersecurity and GRC. I discovered that the principles I’ve practiced throughout my career—protecting data, assessing risk, enforcing standards, monitoring compliance, and supporting informed decision-making—align closely with the core responsibilities of cybersecurity governance.

Today, I am intentionally building my cybersecurity foundation through structured GRC training, hands-on projects, and certifications such as Security+ and CISA. I am excited to apply my analytical mindset, data governance experience, and compliance background to help organizations strengthen their security posture and make risk-aware decisions.
